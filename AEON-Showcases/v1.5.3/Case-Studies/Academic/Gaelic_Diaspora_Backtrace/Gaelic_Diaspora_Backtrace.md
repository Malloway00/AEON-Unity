# Gaelic Diaspora Backtrace

## Scenario

Perform a reverse-time reconstruction of Gaelic diaspora dialects, simulating linguistic divergence caused by migration:

**Parameters:**
- **Starting point:** Modern Irish, Scottish, and Manx Gaelic varieties
- **Historical event:** Multiple waves of diaspora (17th-19th centuries)
- **Geographic spread:** Ireland → Scotland → Isle of Man → Nova Scotia
- **Goal:** Reconstruct the common ancestor and track divergence patterns

**Required Outputs:**
1. Phonological inventories for each modern variety and the reconstructed proto-form
2. Comparative tables showing systematic sound correspondences
3. At least 5 glossed examples demonstrating morphological retention vs. innovation
4. Plausibility scores for each proposed sound change
5. Analysis of how diaspora trauma affected lexical domains (kinship, land, identity)
6. Assessment of mutual intelligibility between varieties at different time depths

## Systematic Sound Correspondences

### Diaspora Sound Change Patterns

**1. Irish → Scottish Gaelic**
- /x/ → [h] intervocalically
- Word-final vowel reduction
- Glottal stop insertion

**2. Scottish Gaelic → Manx**
- Dental fricative loss
- Vowel centralization
- Consonant cluster simplification

**3. Nova Scotian Gaelic**
- English substrate influence
- Vowel quality modification
- Accelerated phonological erosion
